History

THE YOUTH HOSTEL MOVEMENT, was the brainchild of an ancient German Richard Schirmann, who beleived in the virtues of group excursions to study geography, history and the world of nature. He took delight in organising weeklong trails for students. During an excursion in 1909, one night on 26th August, a terrible ranged with thunder and lightning. While his young wards were fitfully asleep in the school premises, Richard lay awake toying at the idea of utilising the school buildings during holidays. It flashed to him that empty school rooms were waiting to the Youth Hostel Movement. Thus the first youth hostel was opened in 1912 in Altena,Germany. In 1956, Pt.Jawaharlal Nehru summed up the philosophy of youth hos-telling in a very precise manner, with that passion Which characterized his love and concern for youth : "I welcome the growth of the Youth Hostel Movement in India. Apart from the obvious advantages of travel in various parts of the country, there is, I think, a basic significance in men and women doing so in the impressionable years of our lives. We have even more to get some idea of the great development schemes and the changes that are taking place in India. we have to do this not as tourists looking at strange objects but rather as children of India discovering our own country, with a desire to participate in the fashioning of India." The International Youth Hostel Federation was inaugurated in 1932 as an outcome of the First International Youth Hostel Conference held at Amsterdam. At present there are 5600 Youth Hostels in 56 countries covering all the continents.

Hostel Rules & Regulations

  • Hostel is meant for YHA members.Bonafide students and others connected with YHA movement. It is for the inmates to see that the facilities are properly utilized and Hostel property is not damaged. Any damage to Hostel property by them is punishable by the Hostel authorities. Right of admission in the Hostel is reserved .
  • No local person irrespective of his category of YHA membership, studentship or any other consideration, will be allowed to stay overnight in the hostel.
  • Booking through travel agents is prohibited.
  • Mostly Dormitory type accommodation is provided, separately for gents and ladies. The maximum period of stay in the Hostel is three nights which can be extended, at the discretion of the Manager, upto seven nights in deserving cases.
  • Prescribed closed hours during day are 10am to 5pm. In exceptional circumstances inmates may be permitted to stay during the day. Hostel closing time is 10pm.
  • Persons with infections / contagious disease are not allowed to stay in the Hostel
  • No person will be allowed after closing time at night. In exceptional circumstances inmates may enter after closing time with previous permission of the Manager.
  • Uses possession of drugs / alcohol and other intxoicants etc, and gambling in the Hostel premises is strictly prohibited. Smoking in common residential areas is not allowed.
  • Hostellers should not take visitors to the residential areas. They may, however, meet their guests in lounge or other areas.
  • Hostellers should use their own locks on the lockers. Hostel does no take any responsibility for loss of their belongiings.
  • Lights off time is 11 PM.
  • Self-service and performance of any Hostel duty prescribed by Manager is normal practices in Youth Hostel.
